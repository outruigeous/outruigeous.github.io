My process

How I approach design problems.

01

Understand the problem

Before jumping to solutions, I spend time understanding what's actually going on. That means talking to stakeholders, reviewing existing research, and forming a clear point of view on what problem is worth solving and why it matters.

Stakeholder interviews Desk research Problem framing
02

Research with the people who matter

I design with users, not just for them. I use a mix of qualitative and quantitative methods depending on the questions we need to answer — interviews, usability testing, surveys, diary studies, and contextual inquiry.

User interviews Usability testing Surveys Contextual inquiry
03

Synthesise and make sense of it

I turn raw research into something the team can act on. That might be affinity maps, journey maps, service blueprints, or a clear set of insights that challenge the assumptions we started with.

Synthesis workshops Journey mapping Service blueprinting
04

Design, test, iterate

I move from concept to prototype quickly so we can test ideas before investing in them. I prefer low-fidelity first, high-fidelity once the structure is solid. I test early and often, and I'm comfortable killing ideas that don't hold up.

Wireframing Prototyping Usability testing Iteration
05

Ship and learn

Design doesn't end at handoff. I stay close to implementation, track outcomes after launch, and bring what I learn back into the next cycle.

Design handoff Post-launch review Metrics tracking
